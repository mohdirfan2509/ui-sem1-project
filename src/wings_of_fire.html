  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

    Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was a visionary scientist, aerospace engineer, and the 11th President of India, known for his dedication to the nation's development. As the "Missile Man of India," he played a key role in advancing India's missile and space programs, contributing to projects like Agni and Prithvi missiles. His leadership at ISRO and DRDO helped strengthen India's defense and technological capabilities. Beyond science, he was a beloved teacher and mentor, inspiring millions of young minds to dream big and serve the country. His legacy continues to inspire generations through his contributions to education, innovation, and national development.

  • Wings of Fire is the autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, chronicling his journey from a small-town boy in Rameswaram to India's "Missile Man" and later the President. The book highlights his struggles, determination, and contributions to India's space and defense programs. It emphasizes the power of hard work, perseverance, and visionary thinking in achieving success. Through his experiences, Kalam inspires young minds to dream big and contribute to the nation.
